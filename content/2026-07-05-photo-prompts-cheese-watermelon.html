拍照说什么

What Do You Say for a Photo?

拍照 Photos代际差异 Generational difference

父亲说拍照喊“茄子”，孩子却说学校问“西瓜甜不甜”

The father remembers saying "Qiezi" (eggplant) for photos, but the child says school photos use "Xi gua tian bu tian?" (Is the watermelon sweet?)

Download PDF
A-父亲 fatherB-孩子 child
A: 我们来拍一个，茄子、橙子？
wǒ men lái pāi yí gè, qié zi, chéng zi?
Let's take a picture. "Qiezi" (eggplant)? "Chengzi" (orange)?
“茄子”是以前常见的拍照口令，“橙子”是父亲顺口换的水果词。
"Qiezi" (eggplant) was a common photo prompt in Chinese, similar to saying "cheese." "Chengzi" (orange) is another fruit word the father says casually.
B: （不说话）
（ bù shuō huà ）
(Silence.)
A: 为什么拍照会说茄子呢？
wèi shén me pāi zhào huì shuō qié zi ne?
Why do people say "Qiezi" (eggplant) when taking photos?
B: 不知道。
bù zhī dào.
I don't know.
A: 我认为是说茄子跟笑的脸型差不多吧。
wǒ rèn wéi shì shuō qié zi gēn xiào de liǎn xíng chà bu duō ba.
I think the mouth shape for saying "Qiezi" (eggplant) looks a lot like smiling.
A: 你应该在学校拍照过吧？
nǐ yīng gāi zài xué xiào pāi zhào guò ba?
You've had photos taken at school, right?
B: 西瓜甜不甜？
xī guā tián bù tián?
"Xi gua tian bu tian?" (Is the watermelon sweet?)
拍照时会问的问题。
This is the question asked when taking the photo.
B: 甜，也有人回答不甜。
tián, yě yǒu rén huí dá bù tián.
"Tian" (sweet). Some people answer "Bu tian" (not sweet).
